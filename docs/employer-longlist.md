## Notes

- Start with OEMs and O&M contractors — they hire at scale, year-round
- Operators second — many outsource O&M so fewer direct tech roles
- Staffing agencies last — expect duplicates with direct employer listings
- Private companies (Invenergy, Enercon) may not have Workday/Greenhouse/SF — check their careers page first
- Chinese state-owned enterprises (Longyuan, Datang, Huaneng) are unlikely to have accessible Western ATS systems — skip for now
- O&M contractors in Europe often use German-language ATS configurations — worth checking SuccessFactors EU instances

## Open questions (revisit as data grows)

- **Offshore as a separate niche?** Offshore techs (GWO certified, vessel-based) may be a distinct candidate pool from onshore techs. Not clear yet — collect both for now and see if the job titles and candidate behavior diverge. The data will tell us.
- **Training & certification companies?** Bodies like GWO (Global Wind Organisation) and BZEE post jobs too — instructors, assessors, training coordinators. Wind techs actively check these between contracts. Worth adding once we have a clearer picture of whether candidates use the site for that kind of role.
- **Staffing agencies — signal or noise?** They surface smaller operators we'd never find otherwise, but also duplicate direct postings. Worth running one agency (e.g. Airswift) as an experiment to measure duplicate rate before committing to the category.
- **This list is a starting point, not a map.** We don't know the industry well yet. The more employers we onboard and the more jobs we collect, the clearer the shape of the market becomes — who hires at volume, which roles are always open, which companies are growing. Let the data lead.
